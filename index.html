Infectious Disease Stifles Democracy?

Autocracies Thrive Where Disease Rates Are High

What explains why democracy flourishes in some countries but not others? One possible explanation is that areas overwhelmed by illness have a tougher time establishing more egalitarian, participatory governmental systems. This chart illustrates a correlation between the rate of disease and the development of democracy. It suggests a circular outcome: Disease causes autocracy, which in turn causes more poverty, which then results in more disease, producing continued autocracy.

Who's Doing Time in State Prison?

Adults vs. Juveniles: It Depends on the Crime

California adults and juveniles serve nearly identical time for lesser felonies such as burglary. But the more serious and violent the offense, the greater the disparity between the two. Average time served for first-degree murder is more than three times longer for adults than for offenders under age 18.

The Darwins offer dazzling displays of doomed ditziness: the man who tried to cure constipation by inserting a 19-inch eel into his rectum, the wise guy who attempted to club chickens to death with the butt of a loaded gun, the old coot who anchored his boat with a WWII aviation bomb.
